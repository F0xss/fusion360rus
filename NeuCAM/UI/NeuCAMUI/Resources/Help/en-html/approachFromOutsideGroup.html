The Front and Back limits, specified in the Geometry tab, often define the extents of the stock material.

1 - Back limit
2 - Front limit

In some cases, it is important to extend cutting moves beyond the Front limit. For example, when machining a model model that has an inclined surface at the front. The center of the cutting tool would need to be outside of the front limit to cut the end of the model with the edge of the tool.

Beyond Front Limit
Deselected
Edge not fully machined

Beyond Front Limit
Selected
Edge machined

Extending the cutting moves beyond the front limit also increases toolpath safety as it allows the tool to plunge further away from the stock and then safely move into the stock.
