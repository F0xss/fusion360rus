Specifies the distance at which the tool begins to approach the part using the cutting feedrate.

This applies to lead moves and ramping moves. The example below shows the effect of increasing the Entry Distance when using a helical ramp.

Large Entry Distance

Note: If you use a Rapid Distance value of 10 mm for example, and an Entry Distance distance of 3 mm, then 7 mm of the entry move is performed at the rapid feedrate, and the remaining 3 mm at the cutting feedrate.
