Specifies the primary way that the tool should tilt when machining the part.

Vertical - Aligns the tool with the specified tool orientation.

Lead and lean - Tilts the tool axis by specifying lead and lean angles. A lead and lean angle of 0 aligns the tool axis so that it is always perpendicular to the surface it contacts. A lead angle tilts the tool in the direction of the cut. A lean angle tilts the tool sideways from the direction of the cut. An example of when you should use lean and/or lead angles is to tilt the tool axis to avoid cutting with the center of a ball-nosed tool.

From Curve - The tip of the tool points away from a sketched curve.

To Curve - The tip of the tool points towards a sketched curve.

From Point - The tip of the tool points away from a sketched point.

To Point - The tip of the tool points towards a sketched point.
