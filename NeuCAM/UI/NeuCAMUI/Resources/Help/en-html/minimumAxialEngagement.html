Specifies the minimum length of the tool's flute that should be engaged with each cutting move.

Useful for engaging with sufficient material. The tool could experience excessive vibrations if it does not have sufficient material to engage with when cutting.

In the example below, the tool performs a cut at a large depth and then performs finer-step moves in the upwards direction. With no minimum axial engagement limit, all the finer-step moves are machined.

With a Minimum Axial Engagement of 1 mm, any cutting move that causes the tool to engage with less than 1 mm on its flute is removed. The remaining material can be machined with a future operation that engages with more of the stock.
