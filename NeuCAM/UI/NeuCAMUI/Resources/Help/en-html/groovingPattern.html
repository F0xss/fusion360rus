Specifies the strategy used to create the groove toolpath. The geometry you are turning, the material from which your part is made, and the type of grooving insert and holder you want to use determines which option is best to use.

Full Stepdown

Partial Stepdown

Sideways With
Partial Stepdown

Full Stepdown - Removes all the material radially, before moving along the spindle axis to remove the next amount. The amount the tool steps over each time is controlled by the Maximum Grooving Stepover value.

Partial Stepdown - Removes all the material along the spindle axis before starting to remove the material at the next depth specified by the Maximum Groove Stepdown value.

Sideways With Partial Stepdown - In some materials it is better to groove from side to side since it results in smaller chips and increased tool life. For example, if the groove is wider than it is deep, using this option can result in faster overall material removal rate than the Full Stepdown or Partial Stepdown options. Tip: In a case like this, use the Both Ways option in the Direction drop-down menu on the Tool tab, as shown in the above image.
