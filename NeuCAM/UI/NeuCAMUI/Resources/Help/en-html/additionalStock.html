An estimated value used to produce a safer order of machining passes between two nearby steep walls when Steep first is selected as Priority.

When uneven stock material remains from a previous operation between two steep walls, cutting to a large depth on just one of the steep walls creates a heavy load on the tool as it interacts with left-over material around it. It is better to cut alternately between the two steep walls to avoid damaging the tool.

Maximum Remaining Stock is used to reorder the toolpath such that two steep walls near each other are machined at the same Z-level before proceeding to the next Z-level.

Estimate a value that is greater than the distance between toolpath segments around both walls. The tool and model geometry affects when and where the reordering takes place.

Maximum Remaining Stock = 2mm
Steep walls are machined exclusively.

Maximum Remaining Stock = 4mm
Steep walls are machined simultaneously.

In this example, changing the Maximum Remaining Stock value from 2 mm to 4 mm reorders the machining passes.

Note: If no Maximum Remaining Stock value is entered, a default minimum value of 10% of the tool's radius is used.
